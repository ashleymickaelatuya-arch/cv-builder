Expert Advice

Tips for a Job-Winning CV

Hiring managers review hundreds of CVs. These evidence-based tips help yours stand out and pass applicant tracking systems (ATS).

🎯

Tailor It to Every Job

Mirror the language from the job posting. ATS software scores CVs against job descriptions before a human ever sees them. Generic CVs rarely win interviews.

📊

Quantify Achievements

Replace vague duties with measurable results. "Led a team of 8 engineers delivering a $2M product on schedule" beats "managed a team" every time.

✂️

Keep It to 1–2 Pages

Unless you have 15+ years of experience or are applying to academic positions, one page is the gold standard. Anything longer risks being discarded unread.

💼

Lead With Your Strongest Section

Recent graduate? Put Education first. Seasoned professional? Lead with Work Experience. Career changer? A strong Summary reframes your background immediately.

🔡

Use Action Verbs

Start every bullet point with: achieved, built, designed, launched, led, reduced, increased. Avoid passive "was responsible for" — it weakens impact.

🧹

Proofread Ruthlessly

A single spelling mistake can disqualify you instantly. Read your CV backwards to catch errors, ask a friend to review, and spell-check before every send.

How to Set Up Google AdSense

A step-by-step guide to monetize this site once it's published. Follow these steps in order — the whole process takes about 1–2 weeks.

1

Publish Your Site First

Deploy this HTML file to a free host like GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Vercel. Upload the file as index.html. You need a live URL before applying to AdSense. Google won't approve sites that aren't live.

2

Apply at google.com/adsense

Go to google.com/adsense and click "Get Started." Sign in with your Google account, enter your site URL, and provide your payment details. Google needs to know where to send your earnings.

3

Add the AdSense Verification Script

After applying, Google gives you a small <script> tag. Paste it inside the <head> section of your HTML file — above the closing </head> tag. This lets Google verify you own the site. Wait for approval (usually 3–14 days).

4

Replace Ad Placeholder Divs

Once approved, go to AdSense → Ads → By Ad Unit → Create new ad unit. Copy the code snippet. In this HTML file, find the comments that say REPLACE WITH ADSENSE CODE and paste your ad unit code there. There are 2 placements ready: a banner (728×90) above the builder, and a rectangle (300×250) in the middle.

5

Set Up Auto Ads (Optional)

In AdSense, you can also enable Auto Ads — Google's AI automatically finds the best places to show ads on your page. Just paste one script tag in <head> and AdSense handles placement. Good for beginners; manual placement gives more control.

6

Set Up Premium Payments (Stripe or Gumroad)

For the Premium "Remove Ads" feature: create a free account at gumroad.com or stripe.com. Create a product priced at $4.99. Copy your payment link. In this file, find function handlePayment() in the JavaScript and replace the placeholder URL with your payment link. After payment, email the unlock code to buyers.
