Expert Advice

Tips for a Job-Winning CV

Hiring managers scan hundreds of CVs. These evidence-backed tips help yours stand out and pass applicant tracking systems (ATS).

🎯

Tailor It to Every Job

Mirror the exact language from the job posting. ATS software scores CVs against job descriptions before a human ever sees them. Generic CVs rarely win interviews.

📊

Quantify Your Achievements

Replace vague duties with measurable results. "Led a team of 8 engineers, delivering a $2M product on schedule" beats "managed a team" every time.

✂️

Keep It to 1–2 Pages

Unless you have 15+ years of experience or are applying to academic positions, one page is the gold standard. Anything longer risks being discarded unread.

💼

Lead With Your Strongest Section

Recent graduate? Put Education first. Seasoned professional? Lead with Experience. Career changer? A strong Summary at the top reframes your entire background.

🔡

Use Strong Action Verbs

Start every bullet with: achieved, built, designed, launched, led, reduced, increased. Avoid passive "was responsible for" — it weakens impact and bores recruiters.

🧹

Proofread Ruthlessly

A single spelling mistake can disqualify you instantly. Read backwards to catch errors your brain skips, ask a friend to review, and spell-check before every send.
